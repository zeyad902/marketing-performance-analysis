# Marketing Performance Analysis 📊

A business-focused data analysis project that explores marketing performance across channels, campaigns, regions, devices, and lead conversion. The goal was to turn raw marketing data into clear, actionable business recommendations.

## Project Summary
This project analyzes a synthetic marketing dataset with around 3,000 rows across 4 related tables. The work covered data cleaning, KPI calculation, exploratory data analysis, and lead funnel analysis to identify where performance was strong, where budget was being wasted, and where the company could improve decision-making.

## Business Questions
This project answers key business questions such as:
- Where is the marketing budget being wasted?
- Which channels, campaigns, and regions deserve more investment?
- Which campaigns should be repeated?
- Are the leads actually good quality?
- Is the real issue traffic acquisition or lead conversion after acquisition?

## Key Insights
- **Instagram** delivered the strongest ROI while receiving relatively lower spend.
- **Facebook** showed the lowest CPA and balanced performance.
- **Google Ads** consumed the highest spend but did not produce the strongest return.
- **Ramadan Offer** and **Summer Sale** were the strongest campaigns and the clearest candidates to repeat.
- **Tanta** and **Mansoura** stood out as highly efficient regions.
- The biggest issue was not only acquisition. The lead funnel showed clear conversion and follow-up gaps.

## Business Impact
This analysis helped highlight:
- Budget waste and weak allocation decisions.
- Underfunded high-performing opportunities.
- Campaigns and regions that deserve more focus.
- The need to improve lead follow-up before increasing acquisition spend.

## What I Did
In this project, I:
- Cleaned and standardized the data.
- Defined and calculated key KPIs such as CTR, CVR, CPC, CPA, ROI, and ROAS.
- Performed exploratory analysis across channel, campaign, region, device, and time.
- Analyzed the leads funnel to understand where conversions were being lost.
- Flagged data quality issues and translated findings into business recommendations.

## Data Quality Notes
A few important data issues were identified during the analysis:
- ROI values appeared unusually high and likely need business validation.
- Some campaign IDs in the leads data did not match the campaigns table.
- Campaign dates were not reliable for timing analysis.
- Some patterns reflected the synthetic nature of the dataset.

## Why This Project Matters
This project is a good example of how data analysis can support better business decisions. It goes beyond charts and metrics by focusing on the questions stakeholders care about most: where money is being lost, where growth opportunities exist, and what actions can improve performance.
